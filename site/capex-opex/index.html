    6. Key Financial Assumptions and Sensitivities

    Assumption Base Case Downside Upside
    Naira/USD rate ₦1,600/$ ₦2,200/$ (import costs rise) ₦1,200/$ (import costs fall)
    Semiconductor cost inflation 0%/year +15%/year (allocation shortage) -5%/year
    Production ramp-up 30% Year 1; 65% Year 2; 100% Year 3 20% Year 1; 50% Year 2 40% Year 1; 80% Year 2
    Average selling price As above -10% (competitive pressure) +5% (brand premium achieved)
    Energy costs (post solar) ₦48M/year ₦80M/year (grid tariff rises) ₦30M/year (excess solar export revenue)
    Pioneer Status approved YES (5-year CIT holiday) NO (30% CIT applies) YES + dividend exemption

    Most sensitive risk: Naira devaluation. ~60% of BOM cost is USD-denominated imports (semiconductors, cores, capacitors). A sustained ₦2,200/$ exchange rate increases unit BOM cost by ~22% and compresses gross margins from 48% to ~38% on inverters unless selling prices are adjusted.

    Hedging strategy: Foreign currency procurement account at Stanbic IBTC or GTBank; forward contracts on USD purchases for semiconductor orders where available; accelerated localisation programme to reduce USD-denominated BOM share from 60% (Phase 1) to <40% (Phase 3).
